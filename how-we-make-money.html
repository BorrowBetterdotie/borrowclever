Transparency

How we make money

Short answer: right now, we don't — and that hasn't influenced a single ranking.

Today: no lender pays for placement

No lender, bank, credit union, or fintech pays BorrowClever to appear on this site, to rank higher, or to be featured. There is currently no commercial relationship between BorrowClever and any of the providers listed in our comparison tables.

Rankings are always mechanical, not editorial:

  • Personal loans are ranked by total cost of credit — the actual interest paid on €10,000 over 60 months — lowest first.
  • Credit cards are ranked by representative APR, the standardised figure every Irish issuer must publish by law — lowest first.

That's it. There is no manual override, no "featured" slot, and no way for a provider to buy a better position. If a lender's stated rate changes, its position in the table changes with it.

What may change

BorrowClever may in future earn referral commissions when a visitor clicks through to a lender and takes out a product — this is a common way independent comparison sites cover their running costs, and we may adopt it. If we do:

  • Every commercial relationship will be listed on this page.
  • Rankings will remain unaffected. A lender paying a referral commission will not move up the table, and a lender not paying one will not be pushed down.
  • Sponsored or promoted placements, if we ever introduce them, will be clearly labelled as such and kept visually and structurally separate from the ranked comparison.

The ranking methodology — total cost of credit for loans, representative APR for cards — will not change to accommodate a commercial arrangement. If that ever stopped being true, this page would say so.
