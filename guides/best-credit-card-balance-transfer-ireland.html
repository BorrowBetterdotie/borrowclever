Best Balance Transfer Credit Cards in Ireland (2026)

Every Irish card with a balance-transfer intro offer — confirmed from issuer websites and CCPC.ie, June 2026

If you're carrying credit card debt in Ireland, a balance transfer is the only way to service it without paying interest for a period. But the Irish market is small enough that the choice of cards is limited — and not all "balance transfer" offers are actually 0%. This guide lists every live offer, explains the conditions that matter, and tells you what to watch out for when the introductory period ends.

The reduced-rate (non-0%) offers

Two Irish cards offer a reduced rate rather than 0% on balance transfers. These are cheaper than the standard rate but not free:

Card Intro rate Duration Balance cap Revert rate (APR)
BOI Affinity 2.9% fixed 12 months — 22.9%
AIB Platinum 3.83% variable 12 months €5,000 22.9%

A 2.9% or 3.83% rate is still far cheaper than a standard credit card APR of 22.9%, and the 12-month window is longer than most 0% offers. For a large balance that you cannot clear in 6–9 months, a reduced-rate 12-month offer may work out cheaper overall than a 0% offer you have to switch cards to extend.

The conditions that matter before you apply

Transfer window

Most Irish balance transfer offers require you to move the balance within a set window of opening the card — typically 90 days for An Post Classic and Avant. If you miss the deadline, you lose the introductory rate. Open the card and initiate the transfer promptly.

New customer only (Bank of Ireland)

All three BOI cards (Classic, Platinum Advantage, Aer) with 0% balance transfer offers are for new customers only. If you already hold a BOI credit card, you will not qualify for the introductory rate on a new BOI card.

Either/or with the purchase offer (Bank of Ireland)

BOI's 7-month 0% balance transfer offer is an either/or choice with a 0% purchase offer. You can use one or the other, not both. If you want to use the card for new spending at 0%, you cannot also transfer a balance at 0%.

Balance cap (AIB Platinum)

AIB Platinum's balance transfer offer is capped at €5,000. Any balance above €5,000 reverts immediately to the standard rate (22.9% APR).

Cannot transfer between same-issuer cards

You cannot transfer an AIB balance to another AIB card, or a BOI balance to another BOI card. The balance must come from a card at a different institution.

The maths: how much can a balance transfer save?

For illustration: suppose you have a €3,000 balance on a card at 22.9% APR and you switch to An Post Classic with 0% for 12 months.

  • At 22.9% APR on €3,000, you'd pay approximately €690 in interest over 12 months if you make only minimum payments.
  • On the 0% An Post card, you pay zero interest for 12 months — and if you clear the balance in that time, you pay nothing in interest at all.
  • The €30 stamp duty still applies to the new card, so the saving is interest paid minus €30.

The saving is real and substantial. The only risk is failing to clear the balance before the revert date.

What to do when the intro period ends

Plan from day one. If you open a card with a 12-month 0% offer, divide your outstanding balance by 12 and that is the monthly payment needed to clear it at zero cost. Set up a direct debit for at least the minimum payment to protect your credit record — but pay as much as you can above that.

If you reach month 10 and still have a balance, start researching your next move. You can:

  • Switch to another 0% card. Opening another balance transfer card resets the clock. You cannot switch to another card at the same bank, but you can move to a different issuer's 0% offer.
  • Switch to a lower-rate card. If you expect to carry a balance long-term, compare the standard APRs on our credit cards comparison page → and consider moving to a lower ongoing rate.
  • Clear the balance with savings if possible. Guaranteed 0% on card debt is more valuable than any savings interest you'd lose.
