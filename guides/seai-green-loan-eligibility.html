SEAI Green Loan Eligibility Ireland 2026

The full requirements for Ireland's cheapest personal loan rates — from 3.00% APR

Ireland's cheapest personal loan rates — 3.00% to 4.23% APR — are not open to everyone. They are reserved for homeowners who hold a confirmed SEAI home energy grant and are borrowing to fund qualifying energy upgrade works. This page explains exactly what qualifies, what the process involves, and what your alternatives are if you don't meet the criteria.

Two different types of "green loan"

The term "green loan" covers two distinct products in the Irish market, and they work very differently.

SBCI green loans (3.00%–4.23% APR) are subsidised by the Strategic Banking Corporation of Ireland under a scheme linked to SEAI home energy grants. These are the rates at 3.00% (Bank of Ireland), 3.55% (AIB), and 4.23% (PTSB) on our comparison table. They require a confirmed SEAI grant before you can apply, and at least 75% of the loan must go to SEAI-qualifying energy upgrade works.

Standard green personal loans (6.40%–6.50% APR) do not require an SEAI grant. AIB's Green Personal Loan (6.40%) requires 50% or more of the loan to go toward green upgrades. Bank of Ireland's Green Car/Home Improvement Loan (6.50%) covers electric or plug-in hybrid vehicles and energy efficiency works. Neither requires a government grant — but both require you to actually spend the money on qualifying green purposes.

SBCI green loans — who qualifies

To be eligible for an SBCI Home Energy Upgrade loan, you must meet all of the following:

  • You are a homeowner — the property must be your primary or secondary residence in Ireland.
  • You hold a confirmed SEAI home energy grant — the grant must be approved before you apply to the bank. Applications without a confirmed grant will not qualify for the SBCI rate.
  • At least 75% of the loan goes to SEAI-qualifying energy upgrade works — the specific works that qualify are defined by SEAI. See the section below for details.
  • You apply through Bank of Ireland, AIB, or PTSB — these are the three lenders currently participating in the SBCI scheme for home energy upgrades.

What energy upgrade works count as SEAI-qualifying?

SEAI funds a range of home energy upgrade works under its grant schemes. Generally, works that are eligible for an SEAI grant are also qualifying works for the SBCI green loan — but you should confirm the specific scope with both SEAI and your chosen lender before applying.

Works that are typically covered under SEAI home energy grant schemes include:

  • Roof, wall, and floor insulation
  • Heat pump installation (air-to-water, ground-to-water)
  • Solar photovoltaic (PV) panels
  • Solar thermal systems
  • Windows and external doors (as part of a broader works package)
  • Mechanical ventilation with heat recovery (MVHR)
  • Heating controls and smart technology upgrades

The definitive list of qualifying works and current grant amounts is at seai.ie. Grant amounts change — always check the current figures directly with SEAI rather than relying on older information.

Step-by-step: how to get the 3.00% SBCI rate

1
Check your planned works are SEAI-qualifying. Review the current grant schemes at seai.ie and confirm your upgrade works are eligible. The SEAI Better Energy Homes scheme and the One Stop Shop scheme are the two main routes for homeowners.
2
Register and apply for your SEAI grant. You can apply directly through SEAI for the Better Energy Homes scheme, or through an SEAI-registered One Stop Shop provider for a whole-home upgrade. Either way, you need the grant approved before the bank loan application.
3
Receive SEAI grant confirmation. SEAI will issue you with documentation confirming the grant. You will need this when you apply to the bank.
4
Apply for the SBCI green loan at Bank of Ireland, AIB, or PTSB. Present your SEAI grant confirmation. The bank will assess your loan application in the normal way — your credit history, income, and affordability still apply.
5
Use at least 75% of the loan proceeds on qualifying energy works. This is a condition of the SBCI scheme. Using funds for other purposes would be a breach of the loan terms — confirm the exact requirement with your lender.

If you don't qualify for an SBCI loan: your alternatives

If you do not hold an SEAI grant or do not meet the 75% works requirement, you still have green loan options — just at higher rates:

  • AIB Green Personal Loan (6.40% APR) — requires 50% or more of the loan for green upgrades. No SEAI grant needed.
  • Bank of Ireland Green Car/Home Improvement (6.50% APR) — for electric/plug-in hybrid vehicles or energy efficiency home works. No SEAI grant needed.
  • Standard personal loans from 7.20% APR — no green restriction; you can use the funds for any purpose. Compare current rates on our full loans page →

The gap between the SBCI rate and a standard personal loan can be significant. On €10,000 over 5 years, BOI's SBCI rate (3.00% APR) saves approximately €1,385 in interest compared to PTSB's standard rate (7.20% APR). If you are planning major energy works, it is worth pursuing the SEAI grant first before applying for a loan.
